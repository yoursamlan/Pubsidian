Statement:

If $x,y \in \mathbb{R}$ and $x >0$, then there is a positive integer $n$ such that $nx>y$.

Proof:

Given $x,y>0$, let $n=\lceil{\frac{y+1}{x}}\rceil$. Then, \begin{align*} nx&= \lceil {\frac{y+1}{x}} \rceil \cdot x \\ &\ge y+1\\ &>y. \end{align*} $$\tag*{$\square$}$$
